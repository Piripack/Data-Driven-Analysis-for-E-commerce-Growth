Project Overview

Andre Ramos contributed to the Data-Driven Analysis for E-commerce Growth project, which was completed as part of a professional simulation aimed at addressing key business questions raised by senior management.

Project Background

The task was commissioned by the CEO and CMO of Tata Group, who sought to:

  • Understand business performance: Identify key strengths and weaknesses within the company.
  • Uncover growth opportunities: Highlight areas for strategic expansion and revenue generation.
  • Simplify data insights: Create clear, concise visuals to ensure senior management could make well-informed decisions.

Project Scope

The project encompassed:

  • Data Cleaning: Ensuring data accuracy by identifying and excluding invalid values, such as negative quantities and unit prices below ¬£0.
  • Visualisation Creation: Designing visuals that address leadership questions in the simplest yet most effective manner, avoiding misrepresentation.
  • Strategic Insight Delivery: Translating complex data into actionable insights for future business planning.

This project not only emphasised my technical skills in data analysis and visualisation but also highlighted my ability to deliver high-impact insights to senior stakeholders.

Challenges

The online retail store faced several critical challenges that needed to be addressed to empower data-driven decision-making:

1. Seasonal Revenue Trends

The CEO required a clear understanding of monthly revenue trends for the year 2011. This would allow the identification of seasonal patterns, such as months with the highest and lowest revenue, and enable accurate forecasting for the upcoming year.

2. High-Performing Countries

The CMO needed to identify the top 10 revenue-generating countries (excluding the UK) and examine their sales performance based on revenue and quantity sold. Insights into these regions were crucial for focusing on areas that contributed the most to the business and devising growth strategies.

3. Customer Insights

The CMO required an analysis of the top 10 customers by revenue. This was essential for understanding the contributions of high-value customers to the overall business and ensuring that strategies were in place to retain and satisfy these key contributors. Additionally, insights into customer diversification were necessary to mitigate over-reliance on a few customers.

4. Regional Product Demand

The CEO sought insights into product demand across regions (excluding the UK) to identify areas with high growth potential. These insights would help guide strategic expansion and develop targeted marketing efforts to maximise revenue from these regions.

5. Data Quality and Presentation

The dataset had significant quality issues, including negative quantities, unit prices below ¬£0, missing CustomerID values, and duplicate records. The leadership team emphasised the importance of impactful, clear, and concise visualisations to enable quick comprehension of trends and patterns. Misrepresentation or unclear visuals could risk poor decision-making.
